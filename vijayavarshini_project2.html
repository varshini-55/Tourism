Ananthagiri Hills In Andhra Pradesh

With lush greenery on one side of the hills accompanied by cool breezes, Ananthagiri Hills is the perfect destination to get yourself out of the circle of monotony. If you are vexed up with the day-to-day desk jobs; then pack your bags and head over to the beautiful Ananthagiri waterfalls to stay in the lap of nature. As a complement to the breathtaking views, Ananthagiri hills also possess a temple which is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Apart from the temple for the religious people, the hills possess Nagasamudram Lake, which is the perfect place to do some camping. All-in-all, this place is just perfect to enjoy a relaxing weekend. Apart from this, it retains the better part of the culture and cuisine of Andhra Pradesh and has an already established tourism development as opposed to Telangana which has to start from scratch.

How to get to Ananthagiri Hills

By Air:The Rajiv Gandhi International Airport is the closest to the Ananthagiri Hills. From the airport, you can either take a taxi or a bus to reach Vikarabad and hence the Ananthagiri Hills. It will cost you approximately INR 2000-3000 to reach there.
By Rail:Vikarabad railhead is the closest to the Ananthagiri Hills. The hills are approximately 6-7 km away from the railway stations, and it would take you 15- 20 minutes to reach there.
By Road:Hyderabad is connected directly to Vikarabad by State Highway 4 (SH4)uptoManneguda. It will take you 2-3 hours approximately to reach there from the city.
By Bus:There are regular buses from Hyderabad to Vikarabad run by TSRTC, from where you can take an auto or another bus to reach the Ananthagiri Hills. It is most likely recommended to take the roadways, as it is the quickest and the most budget-friendly way to reach the hills in a less amount of time.

About Ananthagiri Hills

One of the most popular choice among the day trippers, this scintillating hill station is one of a kind. Featuring a vast stretch of lush green forests, scenic views, and perfect spots for trekking and adventure sports; Ananthagiri Hills carry a charm of their own. You can relax on the greenery at the night camping and gaze at the stars, or you can even go on an adrenaline rush by taking a short trek over these hills. Ananthagiri was one of the official hunting grounds among the Nizams, owing to which, it is amazingly crafted and planned. Amidst these hills is a quaint Musi river which is the ideal place to do some fantastic camping. Apart from the Musi river, it also has the Kotapally Lake which is pretty close to the hills.

This hill station is in its best position during the monsoons. Several streams and waterfalls flow throughout the hills, attracting tourists from all over. Situated about 6 km away from Vikarabad of the Rangareddy District, the Ananthagiri Hills are the most lovely hills of this region. They are also the home to the Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple which is a famous holy place among the Hindus. Regarded to be one of the oldest-inhabited areas in Telangana, the hills possess an array of ancient monuments and caves that a treat for the sore eyes. In addition, they also have the aromatic coffee plantations that attract the coffee lovers from all over the country.

These hills bear excellent weather throughout the year, making it a place that you can visit anytime that you wish to. It is a home for calm surrounding with least pollution, that will make you feel at peace away from all the hustle and bustle of the city life. The hills offer a significant amount of privacy and safeguard all the people staying there from various kinds of disturbances. Apart from the pristine air and the gorgeous greens, the Ananthagiri Hills are the perfect place to enjoy some solitude and ‘me’ time.

Ananthagiri Hills boasts a good number of viewpoints for you to click some amazing pictures on your cameras. So, don’t forget to carry your cameras when you are going on a trip to these mesmerising hills!

Interesting facts about Ananthagiri Hills

  1. The Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple is one of the most exciting places in the hills that attracts religious tourists from all over India.
  2. The hills consist of two trails that are popular for trekking. These trails lead to the forests, where you can spot some unusual species.
  3. The Musi River which is connected to the most sacred river in the Himalayas is considered to be a holy stream that attracts tourists and religious people alike.
  4. There is a vast Ananthgiri waterfall located in this place that flows from a great height, offering some scintillating views from the top.
  5. It is the perfect place to go on trekking for the first time trekkers, as it is safe and comfortable, making the experience a memorable one.
  6. There is a large congregation of ancient caves and forts that attract people who love history and arts.

Location

Anantagiri is the located in the ranges of the Eastern Ghats at a distance of 40 km from Vishakhapatnum. In fact Anantagiri is located of the top of the Elysian Tirumala Hills approximately 17 km from Araku valley, which is 112 kms from Vishakhapatnum.

Best Time To Visit

Owing to the lovely landscape Anantagiri experiences excellent weather all through the year. Thus you may plan your holiday at the hill resort of Anantagiri any time you wish too. The salubrious weather and the calm surroundings add to the beauty of Anantagiri and make it popular with the privacy seekers honeymooners too

Tourists Attractions

Along with the pristine air and gorgeous greens the entire ascent of Anantagiri hills is filled with sights and smells that attract tourists.

There are the many captivating coffee plantations that offer an enthralling sight at Anantagiri. Imagine the pleasure the soul of the tourists derive from the wonderful waterfalls, ravishing ravines and the gorgeous gorges that dot Anantagiri. The mango groves and the many assorted orchards wrap Anantagiri in their shade. Several swift streams flow through Anantagiri though it is the river Musi or Muchkunda that originates from Anantagiri.

Places Around Anantagiri

The breathtakingly beautiful Borra caves near Vishakhapatnum make an excellent excursion option from Anantagiri. The natural formation of the limestone Borra Caves is unique and has attracted tourists since long. The Borra Caves are themselves one-million-year-old.
